## Design Context

### Users
The Atelier is used by households managing clothing and tailoring measurements for multiple family members. It is a calm reference tool people open during fitting, wardrobe planning, and garment ordering, usually on a laptop or tablet at home. The job to be done is keeping measurements organized, legible, and easy to update without the interface feeling clinical or utilitarian.

### Brand Personality
The brand should feel editorial, exacting, and intimate. It should evoke the confidence of a fashion workbook, the care of a tailor's archive, and the calm of a well-composed fitting room. Typography should do most of the emotional work, making the product feel more like a private style ledger than a generic form app.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a light, parchment-toned interface with fashion-book typography and restrained gold accents. The product should feel closer to a lookbook or atelier record than to a dashboard. Favor elegant contrast between headline and body faces, generous line-height for guidance copy, and numerals that feel ceremonial when a measurement is in focus.

### Design Principles
1. Let typography carry hierarchy before adding more containers or decoration.
2. Make measurement values feel archival and important, not merely functional.
3. Keep body copy composed and readable, with calm spacing and disciplined label styles.
4. Use editorial drama selectively: headlines and metrics can be expressive, controls and guidance should stay precise.
5. Preserve warmth and trust through light surfaces, softened contrast, and consistent reading rhythm.
