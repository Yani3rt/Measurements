# Measurement Line Hierarchy Design

## Goal

Make the actual measurement paths stand out clearly on the technical plate even when nothing is selected, while keeping label leader lines readable but secondary.

## Approved Direction

Create a three-level visual hierarchy:

- unselected measurement paths use continuous warm gold-brown strokes
- label leader lines remain dashed and cooler blue-gray
- selected measurements keep the strongest hero treatment with brighter gold and more emphasis

## Visual Roles

### Measurement paths

These should read as the subject of the interface.

- solid strokes
- warmer atelier gold-brown color
- slightly heavier weight than leader lines
- visible enough to read before interaction

### Label leader lines

These should read as annotation.

- dashed strokes
- cooler blue-gray color family
- lighter weight than measurement paths
- quieter opacity so they support without competing

### Selected state

The active measurement should remain unmistakable.

- brightest gold stroke
- strongest opacity
- heaviest weight
- keep the existing pulse and active-path emphasis

## Constraints

- preserve the editorial, fashion-book tone
- do not make the plate feel loud or diagrammatic
- keep measured and unmeasured callouts legible without collapsing the hierarchy

## Success Criteria

- users can instantly distinguish measured geometry from label connectors
- the technical plate reads as a measurement tool first, annotation system second
- selected state still wins clearly over all inactive states
